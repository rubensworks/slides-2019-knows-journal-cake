OSTRICH: a tool for RDF versioning

  • Project: COMBUST (icon)
  • Goal: Efficient storage and querying of Linked Data through a TPF interface
  • Research: Storage technique, query algorithms
  • Development: OSTRICH (C++), OSTRICH-Node (JavaScript)
  • Final article: 35 pages
    (related work: 5, approach: 11, evaluation 4, figures: 13)

PoDiGG: a tool for generating public transport datasets

  • Project: HOBBIT (H2020)
  • Goal: Realistic mimicking of public transport datasets for benchmarking
  • Research: Mimicking algorithm based on population distributions
  • Development: PoDiGG (JavaScript)
  • Final article: 22 pages
    (related work: 3, approach: 8, evaluation 4, figures: 3)

Disclaimer: my opinions

Lessons learned

  • Journal publication is a slow process.
  • Writing for journals is more rewarding than writing for conferences.
  • JWS is more professional than SWJ.
  • Reviewers always want you to do more.
  • Reviewers always want you to write less.
  • Reviewers contradict each other.
  • You can say no to reviewers.
